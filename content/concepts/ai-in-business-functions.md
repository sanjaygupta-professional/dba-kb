# AI in Business Functions

Applied-AI landscape walked through in [[course-03-session-01-transcript|Course_03 Session 01]] and echoed in [[course-04-session-01-20250920-overview|Course_04 Session 01]]. Used to help DBA candidates locate a research topic in their industry.

| Function | Application examples |
|---|---|
| **Compliance** | Real-time monitoring of regulatory changes; AI-driven compliance checks |
| **Finance** | Fraud detection, cost–benefit analytics, security monitoring, agentic-AI for ops |
| **HR** | Employee-experience analytics, skill-gap identification, training recommendation, candidate recommendation |
| **Insurance** | Underwriting with GenAI — mentioned as an area with 3–4 active DBA learners |
| **Customer experience** | AR/VR interfaces, personalized journeys |
| **Operations** | Predictive maintenance, forecasting, anomaly detection |

## Concrete example: fraud detection

See [[course-04-session-01-20250920-overview|Dr. Pappu's live demo]] — a fraud-detection prototype built via [[concepts/vibe-coding|vibe coding]] with both [[concepts/ai-paradigms|Agentic AI and Composite AI]] paths for comparison.

## Research framing

For each application, a DBA candidate should be able to defend the paradigm choice and method — see [[concepts/doctoral-research-methodology|doctoral research methodology]].

## AI strategy layer

Knowing *where* AI applies in a business function is only half the picture. [[concepts/ai-project-strategy|AI Project Strategy]] (Course 07, Prof. Morty) provides the framework to move from a business function observation to a well-formed strategic objective with quantified value. Without this layer, "AI in compliance" remains AI Crush — enthusiasm without a plan. ^[inferred]
