**Instructor**: [[dr-srinivasa-varadharajan|Dr. Srinivasa Varadharajan (Rajan)]]

**Course**: Deep Learning and Variants (Course 05)

Opening session of the Deep Learning course. Frames the motivation for deep learning relative to classical ML, traces the history of ANNs, and lays out the course structure.

## What is Deep Learning?

Deep Learning is ML on **unstructured data** (images, text, audio, video) using Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs) with many layers. Inspired by biological neural architecture but not biologically faithful — a mathematical abstraction.

**Why now?** Three enablers: (1) massive datasets, (2) multi-core GPU compute, (3) algorithmic breakthroughs (attention mechanisms, better optimizers). ANN parameter scale has grown from 5–10 (1955) to 1B–3T (2024).

## Biological Neuron Analogy

- Dendrites → weighted inputs (xᵢ)
- Cell body → summation + activation function
- Axon → output signal
- Synapse strength → weight (wᵢ)
- Hebbian learning: "neurons that fire together, wire together" — basis for weight updates

## ANN Historical Milestones

| Era | Parameters | Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| 1955 | 5–10 | First perceptron |
| 1985 | 50–500 | Backpropagation |
| 2005 | 5K–50K | Deep architectures emerge |
| 2015 | 500K–10M | ImageNet breakthroughs |
| 2020 | 50M–200M | BERT, GPT-2 |
| 2024 | 1B–3T | GPT-4, Gemini |
