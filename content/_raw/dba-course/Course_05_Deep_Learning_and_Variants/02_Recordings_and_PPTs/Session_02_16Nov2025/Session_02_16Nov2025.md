## Key Takeaways

**Topic:** Perceptron architecture formalised; multi-layer networks as a response to XOR

1. **Hebbian Learning** `[05:50]` — Synaptic strength grows with input frequency.
2. **Weights and Synaptic Strength** `[06:42]` — Primary learnable parameters.
3. **Transfer Function** `[07:35]` — Linear combination bias + Σ(w·x).
4. **Activation Function** `[07:46]` — Sigmoid, identity, tanh.
5. **Perceptron** `[08:04]` — Weighted inputs + bias + transfer + activation.
6. **Bias Term** `[08:54]` — Constant offset; critical for expressiveness.
7. **Vector Notation** `[10:44]` — Inputs/weights as vectors → scalar-product form.
8. **Scalar Product** `[11:37]` — Dot product for compact weighted summation.
9. **Linear Regression** `[17:35]` — Perceptron with identity activation.
10. **Cost Function** `[33:23]` — Sum-of-squared-errors objective.
11. **Gradient Descent** `[39:06]` — Iterative opposite-gradient step toward min.
12. **Logistic Regression** `[55:27]` — Perceptron with sigmoid for binary classification.
13. **Maximum Likelihood Estimation** `[55:42]` — Maximises observed-data probability.
14. **XOR Problem** `[57:50]` — Single perceptron cannot separate it → motivates MLP.
15. **Hidden Layer** `[64:39]` — Enables nonlinear feature representations.
16. **Multi-Layer Perceptron** `[65:00]` — Stacked layers for complex boundaries.
17. **Likelihood Function** `[109:14]` — Probability of data given parameters.
18. **Convex Function** `[135:06]` — Single global minimum; reliable GD convergence.
19. **Gradient** `[140:12]` — Vector of partial derivatives — steepest ascent direction.
20. **Partial Derivative** `[145:30]` — Rate w.r.t. one variable, others fixed.
