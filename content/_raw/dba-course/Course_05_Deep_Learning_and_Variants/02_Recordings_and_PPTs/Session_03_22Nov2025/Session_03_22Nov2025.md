## Key Takeaways

**Topic:** Deep neural networks, backpropagation, and gradient descent fundamentals

1. **Perceptron** `[03:49]` — Single-neuron recap.
2. **Multi-layer Perceptron** `[03:49]` — Stacked perceptrons for nonlinear problems.
3. **Activation Functions** `[05:20]` — Sigmoid, ReLU, tanh.
4. **Weight and Bias Parameters** `[04:32]` — Learnables: weights scale inputs, bias = background.
5. **Transfer Function** `[05:06]` — Raw pre-activation neuron output.
6. **Gradient Descent** `[17:25]` — Move opposite the gradient by a learning-rate step.
7. **Cost Function** `[15:27]` — Error measure driving training.
8. **Fully Connected Dense Network** `[40:13]` — Every-to-every connectivity.
9. **Deep Neural Network** `[37:23]` — 3+ layers for hierarchical features.
10. **Forward Propagation** `[63:00]` — Inputs flow to output to produce predictions.
11. **Backpropagation** `[121:10]` — Chain-rule gradients backward through layers.
12. **Chain Rule Derivatives** `[72:00]` — Partial-derivative decomposition enabling backprop.
13. **Learning Rate** `[19:00]` — Step-size hyperparameter.
14. **Online Weight Update** `[123:55]` — Per-sample updates — rapid but noisy.
15. **Batch Processing** `[126:01]` — Full-dataset updates — stable but costly.
16. **Mini-batch Gradient Descent** `[128:09]` — Practical compromise default.
17. **Epoch** `[129:41]` — One complete pass over training data.
18. **Vanishing Gradient Problem** `[160:35]` — Sigmoid-derivative decay stalls early-layer learning.
19. **Overfitting** `[163:18]` — Memorising noise instead of patterns.
